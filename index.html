üì± Hanzo Super App Platform

üíâ Cancer-Fighting Nanobots

Our silicon-based nanorobotics platform represents the cutting edge of precision medicine, designed to revolutionize cancer treatment through targeted, minimally invasive intervention.

Core Capabilities

  • Targeted Drug Delivery: Navigate bloodstream to deliver chemotherapy directly to cancer cells, minimizing side effects
  • Cancer Cell Detection: AI-powered recognition of cancerous tissue through biomarker identification
  • Cell Destruction: Mechanical or thermal methods to destroy cancer cells at cellular level
  • Immune System Support: Enhance body's natural cancer-fighting capabilities
  • Real-Time Monitoring: Track treatment progress and adjust therapy in real-time

Research Collaboration

Development in partnership with RIKEN's supercomputing facilities and Center for Biosystems Dynamics Research. Access to Fugaku and upcoming FugakuNEXT systems enables molecular dynamics simulations and biocompatibility testing.

Timeline: R&D Phase 2026-2028, Clinical Trial Preparation 2028-2030, Regulatory Approval Process 2030+

üõ°Ô∏è Aether A1 G-Repair

G-Repair (Project Name A1) represents groundbreaking nanotechnology in DNA repair, currently in research phase with working prototype projected for Q2 2028.

Key Focus Areas

  • Targeted Delivery: Precisely delivering DNA repair enzymes or activating factors to damaged cellular sites
  • DNA Repair Mechanisms: Designing nanostructures to support or enhance natural repair pathways such as base excision repair (BER) and nucleotide excision repair (NER)
  • Genomic Integrity: Aiming to restore and maintain cellular function by addressing DNA damage at its source
  • Age-Related Applications: Potentially mitigating age-related decline or disease progression

While specific technical details remain proprietary, G-Repair technology focuses on leveraging advanced nanotechnology to enhance DNA repair processes, offering potential applications in preventative medicine and therapies for genetic disorders.

üèóÔ∏è Hanz≈ç C.E.M (Construction & Manufacturing)

The Hanz≈ç C.E.M model revolutionizes construction and manufacturing through advanced robotics, addressing Japan's critical labor shortages while enhancing safety and productivity.

Key Features

  • Quadrupedal Locomotion: Four legs provide superior stability on uneven construction terrain
  • Heavy-Duty Capacity: Lift and transport heavy materials, reducing strain on workers
  • SLAM Navigation: Advanced mapping for complex construction sites
  • Modular Tool System: Switch between drills, saws, welding equipment, grippers
  • 360¬∞ Vision System: Comprehensive site monitoring and hazard detection
  • Industrial Durability: Ruggedized design with shock absorption, dust/moisture resistance
  • Safety Systems: Collision avoidance, emergency stop, proximity sensors
  • IIoT Integration: Remote monitoring and predictive maintenance

Built with aluminum alloys, carbon fiber composites, high-strength steel, HDPE, silicone rubber feet, and ABS/polycarbonate housing for maximum durability and performance.

Development Partners

Leveraging AIST's HRP-5P construction humanoid robot experience and partnering with major construction firms for real-world testing and deployment.

Timeline: Development begins 2027, testing 2028, construction site trials 2028-2029, commercial deployment 2029
